Capital Gain Tax Guidance

Simple guide to understand property tax and legal ways to save it

1. What is Capital Gain Tax?

When you sell a property for more than what you bought it for, the profit is called Capital Gain. The government charges tax on this profit.

Formula: Capital Gain = Sale Price - Purchase Price - Expenses - Improvements

2. Short Term vs Long Term - Key Difference

Type Holding Period Tax Rate Indexation Benefit
Short Term Capital Gain (STCG) Less than 24 months As per your Income Tax Slab (5% to 30%) ❌ Not Available
Long Term Capital Gain (LTCG) More than 24 months Flat 20% with Indexation ✅ Available
💡 Important: For property, 24 months = 2 years. If you sell after 2 years, you get LTCG benefit with indexation which reduces tax heavily.

3. What is Indexation?

Indexation adjusts your purchase price for inflation using CII (Cost Inflation Index). This increases your cost and reduces taxable profit.

Example:
Bought in 2015 for ₹20L. CII 2015 = 254
Sold in 2024 for ₹50L. CII 2024 = 363
Indexed Cost = 20L × 363/254 = ₹28.58L
Taxable Gain = 50L - 28.58L = ₹21.42L only
Without indexation, gain would be ₹30L. You save tax on ₹8.58L!

4. 3 Legal Ways to Save Capital Gain Tax

A. Section 54 - Buy Another House

  • Sell your residential house and buy another house within 2 years
  • Or construct a house within 3 years
  • Full capital gain exempted if new house cost ≥ gain amount
  • Applicable only for residential property

B. Section 54F - Invest in House

  • Sell any capital asset like land, commercial property, shares
  • Invest the entire sale amount in a residential house
  • Full capital gain exempted
  • You should not own more than one house on date of sale

C. Section 54EC - Buy Govt Bonds

  • Invest your capital gain in NHAI or REC bonds within 6 months
  • Maximum ₹50 lakhs investment allowed
  • Lock-in period: 5 years
  • Interest ~5.25% per year, taxable

5. Other Important Points

  • Section 24: Home loan interest deduction up to ₹2L per year
  • Stamp Duty & Registration: Can be added to purchase cost
  • Brokerage & Legal Fees: Can be deducted from sale price
  • TDS: Buyer deducts 1% TDS if sale price > ₹50L
